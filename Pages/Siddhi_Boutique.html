Siddhi Boutique

✦

Siddhi Boutique is a concept e-commerce platform designed for a real client - my mother - who is launching a business focused on traditional Indian clothing and jewelry. She approached me to design and build a digital storefront that reflects cultural heritage while offering a clear, trustworthy shopping experience. This project documents the UI and UX process from research to ideation to high-fidelity, interactive wireframes.

Overview

Siddhi Boutique is an online destination for traditional Indian fashion, offering blouses, jewelry, sarees, lehengas, scarves, and custom tailoring services. Designed primarily for desktop but fully responsive for mobile, the platform serves fashion‑conscious women ages 30–50+ who appreciate elegance, heritage, and craftsmanship.

The goal was to create a culturally rich, intuitive, and visually cohesive shopping experience that honors the ornate beauty of Indian attire while maintaining modern usability and accessibility standards.

Research

Target Audience

Through initial research, I identified four primary users, each with their own needs, motivations, and behaviors.

  • Women aged 30-50+
  • People who are familiar with Indian attire
  • People who value tradition, quality, and customization
  • People who prefer desktop browsing but expect mobile compatibility

Pain Points

Research revealed several and shared recurring pain points across all user groups:

  • Difficulty finding the right product quickly
  • Lack of clarity around custom tailoring
  • Not enough detail in product imagery
  • Confusion during checkout
  • Trust + authenticity concerns
  • Difficulty comparing products

Analysis

Problem Statement

Users struggle to confidently browse, evaluate, and purchase traditional Indian clothing online due to unclear navigation, inconsistent branding, limited product detail, and a fragmented checkout experience.

Project Goals

The website needed to:

  • Create a cohesive, culturally rich brand identity
  • Build an intuitive, easy‑to‑navigate shopping experience
  • Showcase product craftsmanship through strong visual storytelling
  • Simplify the custom tailoring process
  • Reduce friction in the checkout experience
  • Build trust and credibility throughout the experience
  • Optimize for both desktop and mobile
The challenge was to design a digital presence that felt warm, graceful, and trustworthy while supporting both browsing and personalization.

Case Study Summary

Siddhi Boutique is a culturally rich e‑commerce experience designed to bring the elegance and intricacy of traditional Indian fashion into a modern, intuitive digital platform. Through research, user flows, wireframes, and a fully developed design system, this project solves key user challenges around navigation, product clarity, and custom tailoring by creating a cohesive brand identity, a clear information architecture, and a seamless shopping and checkout journey. The final solution blends ornate visual storytelling with structured UX thinking, resulting in a trustworthy, accessible, and emotionally resonant shopping experience for women seeking both ready‑made and custom Indian attire.

Reflection

Key Takeaways:

  • This project allowed me to blend cultural storytelling with UX design principles, and it pushed me to design with both emotion and clarity. I learned how to translate a rich, ornate, and graceful brand identity into a clean, modern interface without losing its warmth or heritage.
  • It also reminded me how meaningful design becomes when it honors the user's cultural heritage while still delivering a smooth, trustworthy shopping experience.

Next Steps

This project laid the groundwork for a more functional site. The next phase will include:

  • A fully functioning website through Visual Studio Code and GitHub
  • Expanding and finalizing the component library to support future features like wishlists and personaized recommendations
  • Adding annotations and interaction notes to high‑fidelity screens so developers clearly understand behavior, states, and transitions.
  • Conducting more usability testing
