Research

Context Study

People today interact with automated decision systems constantly — from loan approvals to job screenings to rental applications — yet most have little understanding of how these systems work or why certain outcomes occur. To evaluate how well current platforms support users in navigating algorithm‑driven decisions, I analyzed how individuals experience and interpret these opaque processes.

I focused on three key areas:

  • Clarity and transparency of decison explainations
  • Accesiblity of information about underlying algorithms
  • User ability to identify potential bias or unfairness
This study highlighted where users feel informed and empowered and where confusion, opacity, or unmet needs continue to create frustration and uncertaintly.

Target Audience

I identified four primary users:

  • Job seekers
  • Loan and credit applicants
  • Renters
  • Consumers
Each group had distinct motivations, but they all shared a desire for clarity, fairness, and a sense of control.

Pain Points

Across research, players consistently struggled with:

  • Opaque decison making
  • Difficulty overcoming bias
  • Confusing or complex technical language
  • No clear next steps
These pain points became core design targets.

Case Study Summary

Eternal Sigils focused on designing a mobile trading card game that balances strategic depth with accessibility, addressing common player frustrations such as overwhelming tutorials, unclear progression, and limited community features. Through user research, competitive analysis, and iterative design, I developed a concept that supports both competitive and casual playstyles, offering fair progression, intuitive collection and trading tools, and a community‑driven experience. The resulting structure provides a scalable foundation for future development, including high‑fidelity prototyping, usability testing, and expanded gameplay systems, demonstrating how thoughtful UX design can make a traditionally complex genre welcoming and engaging for a wide range of players.

Reflection

Key Takeaways:

  • This project reinforced the importance of designing for a wide spectrum of player needs without compromising clarity or depth. Balancing competitive complexity with casual accessibility required careful prioritization, iterative refinement, and a strong understanding of user motivations. . The research phase was especially valuable—it revealed gaps in onboarding, progression transparency, and community features that directly shaped the design direction.
  • How essential it is to create systems that feel fair, intuitive, and rewarding, regardless of a player’s experience level.

Next Steps

This project laid the groundwork for a more functional site. The next phase will include:

  • Creating clickable, interactive high-fidelity wireframes
  • Conducting usability testing with both casual and competitive players
  • Refining gameplay flows based on feedback
  • Exploring ethical monetization models
  • Preparing the system for cross‑platform scalability
