Overview

This project examines the end-to-end UX and UI design process behind creating a movie review website that centers on my personal opinions, ratings, critiques, and in-depth analyses of films. The overall goal was to design, code, and create a platform that provides insightful, easy-to-navigate, and accessible content for a wide range of movie lovers, ranging from casual viewers to dedicated cinephiles.

Process

The process for this project consists of the following:

Problem Space
Movie lovers often rely on movie sites to decide what to watch, but many platforms either overwhelm users with clutter or lack depth and personality. I wanted to create a site that blends:
  • Deep, thoughtful analyses of films for movie lovers and cinephiles
  • Quick, digestible recommendations for casual users
  • Genre‑specific hubs for fans of sci‑fi, horror, action, and more
  • Streaming‑focused lists for users who watch primarily on Netflix, Hulu, or Disney+
  • Family‑friendly guidance for parents
The challenge was to design an entire system that effectively incorporates and supports all of those needs without becoming visually or structurally overwhelming.
Target Audience and Pain Points
I identified six primary users, each with their own needs, motivations, and pain points.
Project Goals
The website needed to:
  • Inform users through reviews, ratings, and critiques
  • Guide users toward movies that match their interests, mood, or platform
  • Engage users through comments, rankings, and multimedia
  • Support discovery with filters, lists, and genre hubs
Content Strategy
The site's content ecosystem consists of:
  • Homepage with featured reviews, lists, and news
  • Movie Reviews
  • Film Genres
  • Best & Worst Rankings (monthly + yearly)
  • Lists & Guides (streaming picks, genre spotlights, film series overviews)
  • Movie News (trailers, clips, interviews, award predictions, box office updates)
  • Arya’s Top 20 Movies
  • About and Contact pages
Each content type was mapped to a specific user need, ensuring the site feels cohesive.
